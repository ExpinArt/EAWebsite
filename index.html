Brain

Students read a bunch of papers, It's part of the job description. Whenever we're not doing experiments we read, study, and write. We read because we believe it'll help us understand what we're doing or what we should do next, and in many cases that can happen, but similarly, all these information can lead to more confusion. So I draw, I paint, I doodle, I try to create.

Cells

Imagine a chemical that allows us to see the very unit that builds us and everything we know of the living world. A peak into ourselves, a peak into the world of cells.

Toxin

Toxin. A word that brings fear, worry, danger. But toxins are not only substances that cause harm to the body. By understanding how they work, we can use their properties to make other chemicals that can be used to study the organs, other toxins, cell signaling, and even make art!

Muse

Nature is a great muse for art. Nature is a great muse for science. In many ways science and art can also draw inspiration from each other. This series shows us that science in its various forms mimic so much of nature. From colors to patterns to feelings.
